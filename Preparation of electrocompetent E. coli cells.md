# Preparation of electrocompetent E. coli cells

# Before starting:
Prepare all required reagents (LB/SOB medium, sterile 10% glycerol)
A pre-cooled centrifuge (4°C) and liquid nitrogen (for flash freeze and long term storage)

***Protocol***
Inoculate 3 mL LB/SOB with a single colony from a fresh plate and grow the culture overnight at 37°C
Inoculate 100 ml SOB with 1 ml of the preculture (use a ratio of 1:100 for the inoculation) and grow it at 37°C, 200 rpm until the OD600 ~ 0.4-0.5 (takes approximately 4 h); continue working on ice or at 4°C for all steps from here on!
Centrifuge the cells at 4000 rpm / 20 min / 4°C
Wash the cell pellet with 1 volume (100 ml) of sterile 10 % glycerol
Centrifuge the cells at 4000 rpm / 20 min / 4°C
Wash the cell pellet with 0.5 volumes (50 ml) of sterile 10 % glycerol
Centrifuge the cells at 4000 rpm / 20 min / 4°C
Wash the cell pellet with 0.1 volumes (10 ml) of sterile 10 % glycerol
Centrifuge the cells at 4000 rpm / 20 min / 4°C
Add 0.5 – 1 mL of sterile 10 % glycerol; resuspend the cell pellet
Prepare 100 µl aliquots in 1.5 mL sterile microcentrifuge tubes and freeze them immediately in liquid nitrogen (not needed if using immediately)
Store the electrocompetent cells at -80°C
